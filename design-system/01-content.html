Fundamentals · 01

Content fundamentals

Before we pick a color or a font, we agree on how CliqueZoom talks. Brazilian photographers can smell a generic SaaS from a kilometer away — and they scroll past it. Our copy has to sound like a photographer wrote it, not a growth team.

1.1 Voice

Three words.

Every piece of copy CliqueZoom writes has to pass three filters. If a sentence fails one, rewrite it.

Warm, not chummy

We treat fotógrafos like peers — adults running a business. We use second-person ("você configura…") but we don't high-five them. No emojis in product copy. No exclamation in body text.

Clear before clever

Labels describe what the button does. A CTA called "Criar conta grátis" beats "Começar sua jornada". Portuguese is direct — we honor that.

Confident, not loud

We don't promise "revolução". We describe what exists. "Seus clientes selecionam as fotos favoritas diretamente no site" — fact, not slogan.

1.2 Voice in practice

Three sentences, one voice.

Hero · landing page
Site, seleção de fotos e entrega online. Tudo em 1 só lugar.
The italicized tail carries the promise. Serif italic is the signature move — use it sparingly (one per page, always in the hero or a section title).
Section lede · pricing
Comece grátis e evolua conforme sua necessidade. Sem surpresas.
"Sem surpresas" is reassurance, not marketing. Every plan page must end with a factual line about what happens when you cancel.
Empty state · admin
Você ainda não tem sessões. Crie a primeira para começar a receber fotos de clientes.
Empty states name the thing that's missing and the single next action. Never "Oops" or "Nada por aqui!".

1.4 Never

Things we never write.

Avoid
  • "Revolucione seu negócio"
  • "Simples assim!" / "Muito fácil!"
  • "A plataforma #1 para fotógrafos"
  • Emoji in product UI (only in onboarding emails, sparingly)
  • ALL-CAPS for emphasis in body text
  • "Desbloqueie", "Turbine", "Alavanque"
Prefer
  • "Seus clientes selecionam as fotos direto no site"
  • "Leva menos de 2 minutos"
  • "Projetado para fotógrafos"
  • Iconography + clear label pairs
  • Italic serif for emphasis in display type
  • "Configure", "Envie", "Organize"
1.5 Density

Length rules.

Hero headline
Max 12 words. Must contain one concrete noun (site, seleção, entrega).
Hero subhead
Max 22 words. One sentence. No semicolons.
Section title
Max 6 words. Sentence case. Period only if multi-clause.
Feature card title
2–4 words. Noun phrase, never a verb.
Feature card body
Max 22 words. Answer "what does this do for me?" in one breath.
Button label
2–4 words. Verb-first. "Criar conta", "Ver exemplo", "Assinar Basic".
Empty state
1 sentence naming the missing thing + 1 sentence with next action.
Error message
Plain language. Never a code. Always tell the user what to do next.
1.6 Localization

It's Brazilian Portuguese, not "Portuguese".

The product is pt-BR first. Spelling follows current Brazilian orthography (no trema, no "ideia" acute, no "linguiça" trema). Avoid Portugal-specific vocabulary.

Dates: DD/MM/AAAA. Prices: R$ 1.299,00 (period as thousand separator, comma as decimal). Times: 24h, never AM/PM.
Formal vs. informal: use "você", never "tu". In legal/billing copy, the register shifts slightly formal — "Você poderá cancelar" becomes acceptable.
